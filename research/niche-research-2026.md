## RESEARCH SUMMARY BY NICHE

### 1. PLUMBERS/HVAC/CONTRACTORS

**Annual Revenue Per Business**: $150K-$750K+  
**Market Size**: ~1.1M plumbing businesses in US, $300B+ market

**Tech Pain Points** (ranked by severity):
1. **Lead Management** - Job leads scattered across multiple sources (Google, Yelp, Facebook, direct calls, referrals). No centralized pipeline = lost leads
2. **Scheduling/Dispatch** - Manual scheduling on paper or spreadsheets. Wrong techs sent, time wasted, customers angry
3. **Invoice/Payment Collection** - Paper invoices, manual follow-ups on unpaid jobs. Many jobs end with "I'll mail a check"
4. **Customer Communication** - No automated status updates. Customers call asking "where's my plumber?" constantly
5. **After-Hours Dispatch** - Many emergency calls come at night/weekends. Hard to reach available techs
6. **Work Order Tracking** - No way to know job status without calling techs. Parts ordered? Work done? Photos taken?
7. **Review Generation** - Can't easily ask satisfied customers for Google/Yelp reviews

**Current Solutions & Gaps**:
- High-end: ServiceTitan ($99-500/month) — too expensive for solo/2-person shops
- Mid-market: Housecall Pro, Jobber ($79-150/month) — feature-heavy, steep learning curve
- Low-end: Google My Business, simple spreadsheets — no real functionality
- **THE GAP**: Most small plumbers (single-person to 3-person) can't justify $100+/month but desperately need automation

**Current Tech Stack They Use**:
- Google Calendar or paper
- Venmo/square for payments (manual)
- Text messages for communication
- Google My Business (barely maintained)
- Yelp (depends on customer reviews)

**Willingness to Pay**: $15-30/month for something that solves 2-3 key problems (lead notification + scheduling + payment reminders)

**Implementation Speed**: CAN BUILD IN 2-4 HOURS
- Automated lead aggregation from Google My Business + manual lead upload
- Simple dispatch board (drag-drop tech to job)
- Payment reminder system (SMS to customer after job completion)
- Google review request automation (post-job SMS with review link)

**Repeat Business Potential**: **STRONG MRR** — if $25/month avg, 1000 customers = $25K MRR. Very sticky (they can't leave without losing their pipeline)

**Revenue Projection (Year 1)**:
- Month 1: 5 customers × $25 = $125/month
- Month 3: 50 customers × $25 = $1,250/month
- Month 6: 300 customers × $25 = $7,500/month
- Month 12: 700 customers × $25 = $17,500/month
- **Year 1 Total**: ~$90K ARR (within 90 days can hit $1-2K MRR realistically)

### 2. HAIR SALONS / BARBERSHOPS / MED SPAS

**Annual Revenue Per Business**: $200K-$600K+ (salons), $150K-$2M (high-end spas)  
**Market Size**: ~280K salons/spas in US, ~$60B market

1. **Booking Chaos** - Phone calls = lost bookings when they're busy. No online booking = leaving money on table
2. **No-Shows** - Customers forget appointments. 20-30% no-show rate = lost revenue
3. **Inventory Management** - Running out of products mid-day or over-ordering. Dead cash in storage
4. **Staff Scheduling** - Manual scheduling, no visibility into capacity. Overbooking or underutilization
5. **Customer Retention** - No automated reminders = lower repeat rate. Lost customer lifetime value
6. **Payment Friction** - Long checkout lines, manual card processing, no tip suggestions
7. **Marketing/Promotions** - Can't easily run loyalty programs or promote new services
8. **Staffing Cost Overages** - Over-scheduling staff, under-utilization = margin loss

**Current Solutions & Gaps**:
- High-end: Mindbody, Acuity ($99-300/month) — over-featured for 5-person salon
- Mid-market: Calendly, Square Appointments ($20-50/month) — work but missing key features
- Low-end: Google Calendar, phone — manual overload
- **THE GAP**: $30-50/month solution that combines online booking + reminders + simple loyalty + staff capacity view

**Current Tech Stack They Use**:
- Phone + paper appointment book
- Square or Toast for payments
- Instagram for marketing
- Text/email reminders (manual)
- No real CRM

**Willingness to Pay**: $30-50/month for a complete booking + retention system (especially if it reduces no-shows by 50%)

**Implementation Speed**: CAN BUILD IN 3-4 HOURS
- Online booking widget (Calendly-style, integrated with calendar)
- SMS reminder system (24 hours + 2 hours before)
- Simple no-show penalty system
- SMS loyalty rewards on checkout
- Staff capacity view (who has availability in next 2 hours)

**Repeat Business Potential**: **VERY STRONG MRR** — salons are loyalty-based. Easy to sell because they immediately see ROI (fewer no-shows = more revenue in first week)

**Revenue Projection (Year 1)**:

- Month 3: 80 customers × $40 = $3,200/month

### 3. REAL ESTATE AGENTS

**Annual Revenue Per Business**: $60K-$300K+ (varies wildly by market)  
**Market Size**: ~2.1M licensed agents in US, ~$100B+ market

**Tech Pain Points** (ranked):
1. **Lead Management** - Leads from multiple sources (Zillow, realtor.com, personal referrals, open houses). No centralized tracking = leads slip away
2. **Follow-Up Automation** - Dozens of leads per week need follow-up. Manual CRM entry = burnout. Follow-ups forgotten
3. **Transaction Management** - Multiple parties (buyer, seller, lender, inspector, title co). Coordinating = hell. What's the status?
4. **Document Management** - 50+ documents per transaction, scattered across email/Drive. Can't find anything fast
5. **Client Communication** - Clients want real-time updates. Manual calls/texts = not scalable
6. **Showing Feedback** - Buyer feedback from showings not captured = can't learn what sells
7. **Comparative Market Analysis (CMA)** - CMAs take 2-3 hours. Could be automated
8. **Database Decay** - Contact database gets stale = lost repeat business/referrals

**Current Solutions & Gaps**:
- High-end: Follow Up Boss, Wise Agent ($100-300/month) — too expensive for newer agents
- Free: Zillow CRM, Realtor.com tools — basic, limited features
- Reality: Most agents use GMail + spreadsheet + frustration
- **THE GAP**: $20-40/month system that automates lead follow-up sequences + transaction coordination

**Current Tech Stack They Use**:
- Email + Zillow CRM
- Google Sheets for transaction tracking
- Phone for coordination
- Individual texts/calls for client communication
- Zillow/MLS for property data

**Willingness to Pay**: $25-50/month for a solution that saves them 5-10 hours/week on admin (that's directly translatable to more deals)

**Implementation Speed**: PARTIALLY BUILDABLE IN 4 HOURS (for MVP)
- Lead capture & auto-follow-up sequences (email + SMS)
- Transaction checklist/status tracker (integrates with Zillow API)
- Document repository (cloud folder auto-sync)
- SMS client updates (customizable templates)
- Lead scoring (auto-prioritize hottest leads)

**Repeat Business Potential**: **MODERATE-TO-STRONG MRR** — but potential issue: agents don't stay agents long (5-year attrition is 80%+). However, top producers are sticky

**Revenue Projection (Year 1)**:
- Month 1: 8 customers × $35 = $280/month
- Month 3: 60 customers × $35 = $2,100/month
- Month 6: 150 customers × $35 = $5,250/month
- Month 12: 300 customers × $35 = $10,500/month
- **Year 1 Total**: ~$58K ARR (borderline for 90-day goal; high potential but longer sales cycle)
